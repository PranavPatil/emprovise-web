Photoshop CS4 

Exploring the Dimensions

Photoshop Workspace

  • menu bar - It runs across the top of the Photoshop window, and contains various menu options for Photoshop's tools.
  • options bar - The options bar sits beneath the menu bar and holds contextualized options for different tools. It also contains the workspace menu, where we can save and load arrangements of palettes.
  • toolbox - By default, the toolbox sits to the left of your Photoshop window, and contains shortcuts to Photoshop tools.

Workspace Continued

  • palettes - These are individual "panes" that hold information or options for working with the file and float on the right-hand side. Each palette is labeled with a tab, and can be minimized, closed, grouped with other palettes, or dragged in and out of a panel dock.
  • document windows - Each open document has its own document window with a status bar along the bottom. The status bar sits to the right of the zoom percentage displayed in the bottom left-hand corner, and displays information that's specific to the document.

Image types

  • GIF 
  •      1)  The GIF format can have a maximum of 256 colors.  
  •      2)  They support transparency and animation
  •      3)  Work best with graphics that have large areas of the  
  •            same color.
  •  
  • JPEG 
  •      1)  It works best with images that have more than 256 
  •            colors and gradients.  
  •      2)  Images saved in JPEG format are compressed, which 
  •           means that image information will actually be lost, 
  •           causing the image to degrade in quality.

PNG Image Format

  • Similar to the GIF format.
  • Supports transparency and works best with solid-color images.
  • Superior to the GIF format as it has the ability to support true levels of transparency for colored areas
  • PNGs can produce a better quality image at a smaller file size than can GIFs

Image Graphics

  • Vector graphics is the use of geometrical primitives such as points, lines, curves, and shapes or polygons, which are all based on mathematical equations, to represent images in computer graphics.
  • Raster graphics image or bitmap is a data structure representing a generally rectangular grid of pixels, or points of color, viewable via monitor, paper etc.
  • Rasterization is the task of converting an image in vector graphics format (shapes) to raster image format.

Photoshop Layers

  • A layer is simply one image stacked on top of another.
  • It allows to work on one part of an image without disturbing the rest of it.
  • The transparent parts of any layer, are shown by the checkered grid, allow the layers beneath that layer to show through.
  • To show or hide each layer in an image click on its corresponding eye icon in the Layers palette.
  •  A layer group is signified by a folder icon

Sample Layers

Working with Layers

  • Rename layers by double-clicking on the layer name.
  • Change transparency of a layer by changing its opacity with Opacity slider.
  • Duplicate a selected layer by pressing Ctrl-J .
  • Rearrange layers by dragging the layer above or below other layers
  • Create new layer by pressing Shift-Ctrl-N or by pressing          icon in layer panel.

Layer Mask

  • It allows to control a layer's level of transparency.
  • The Opacity option in layer panel changes the transparency level for entire layer at once while layer mask could change opacity for parts of the layer.
  • To add a layer mask, press         icon at the bottom of the layer panel.

Layer Styles

  • Layer styles are special effects that can be applied to an entire layer overall to o drastically change the appearance of something in very little time.
  •  Layer styles include a variety of lighting effects, textures and overlays.
  • They can be preset, customized, or even saved and used for later.
  • To create new layer style press         in       layer panel

Closer look at ToolBox

  • The tool icons have small black triangles in their bottom right-hand corners having more tools of corresponding type.
  • On clicking of the tool icon and holding it down, a "flyout" menu will appear, displaying the additional tools.
  • Selections can have varying levels of transparency, known as the degree of opacity
  • Selections could be "feathered" by specifying a radius using Feather field.
  •  

Marquee and Lasso Tools

  • Marquee tools (M) are used to create rectangular or elliptical selections.
  • Single Column, Single Row, Elliptical, Rectangular Marquee Tools.
  • Lasso tools (L) are used to create freeform (asymmetrical shape) selections
  • Magnetic Lasso, Polygonal Lasso, Lasso Tools.
  • Once made a selection, only pixels within the selection can be worked on.

Magnetic Lasso Tool

  • Magnetic Lasso Tool is found with the Lasso Tool in the toolbox.
  • Magnetic Lasso Tool provides help in making selections by following the edges of contrast and color difference.
  • Close the magnetic lasso selection by pressing Enter key or clicking at a point near the beginning of the selection.
  •  
  •  

Magic Wand Tool

  • The Magic Wand Tool (W) selects areas of similar color.
  • We could choose the selection to be contiguous (pixels) or not (matching colors across entire document).
  • Hold shift key to add new selections and alt key to subtract them.
  • Hold shift-alt to intersect selections.
  • Use the arrow keys to move the selection pixel by pixel.
  • Press ctrl-J to copy and shift-ctrl-J to cut selections.

Move     and Crop Tool

  • Move Tool (V) moves a selected area or an entire layer.
  • Invoke Move Tool temporarily while using other tools by holding down the Ctrl key.
  • In order to duplicate the layer hold down the alt key while using the move tool.
  • Crop Tool (C) is used to trim images.
  • Create a selection using the Crop Tool, then double-click the center of the selection, or press Enter, to crop the image to the size of the selection.

Drawing Tools

  • The Brush Tool is used for soft-edged painting or drawing.
  • Pencil Tool is suitable for hard-edged drawing or painting.
  • The Eraser Tool removes pixels from the canvas. 
  • The Paint Bucket Tool fills a selection with a flat color by clicking on the area to fill.

Gradient Tool

  • The Gradient Tool fills a selection with a blend of two or more colors, known as a gradient. 
  • The small triangle on the right-hand side of the Gradient Tool displays the gradient presets and tools.
  • Choose the desired colors, gradient style, and apply it by clicking and dragging the cursor over the area to be filled.

Gradient Styles

  • When the tool is selected, the Gradient options appear on the Options Bar.
  •  
  • The Buttons represent gradient styles. 
  •  
  • The styles represented by buttons are
  •      1) Linear
  •      2) Radial
  •      3) Angular
  •      4) Reflected
  •      5) Diamond
  •  
  • For horizontal gradients, draw a vertical selection line and For vertical gradients, draw a horizontal selection line

Text and Shape Tools

  • The Text Tool, true to its name, creates text layers.
  • Shapes can be created by clicking and dragging tools such as Rectangle, Rounded Rectangle, Ellipse, Polygon, Line, and Custom Shape tools.
  • Set foreground and background colors by clicking on the appropriate tile and choosing a color from the Color Picker

Eyedropper, Hand, Zoom Tool

  • The Eyedropper Tool allows to sample another color from the image, and set it as the foreground color. It could sample colors from anywhere in the display and even from other applications outside Photoshop. 
  • Hand Tool moves on the canvas to look around the picture.
  • Zooming into the image is achieved using the Ctrl + while Ctrl - is used for zooming out of the image.
  •  

History Brush Tool

  • It allows you to paint and reveal previous states of images.
  • The tool makes a copy of the image as it was in a previous state, then uses the content of this copy to paint with in a new state.

Pen Tool

  • Enables to create lines and curves that can be put together custom shapes.
  • The standard Pen tool draws with the greatest precision.
  • The Freeform Pen tool draws paths as drawn with pencil on paper.
  • The magnetic pen option draws a path that snaps to the edges of defined areas in the image. 

Dodge, Burn and Sponge Tools

  • The dodge tool is used to lighten the highlights and bring out the details of the curtains behind the window.
  • The burn tool is used to darken the areas of the image.
  • Dodge and Burn tools are based on a traditional photographer’s technique for regulating exposure on specific areas of the image.
  • The sponge tool changes the level of saturation of colors for an area of an image

Clone Stamp Tool

  • Clone stamp tool is used to duplicate a portion of an image and reapply it repeatedly to cover an unwanted portion of other image.
  • Select the tool, then in target image select the sample piece by left clicking the mouse and holding Alt button to set the sampling point.
  • In the destination image apply by clicking on an area creating a new cloned area.
  • The aligned option allows to clone continuously even after release of mouse and use all layers option makes it visible in all the layers along with current layer.

Slice Tool

  • It allows to divide an image into smaller sections which fit together like a jigsaw (but with straight edges).
  • Slice tool creates the slices and Slice Select tool enables to select and modify the existing slices.
  • On dragging over the area to make into a slice, Photoshop automatically creates the necessary number of slices, with the active slice highlighted.
  • The slice select tool can move and resize slices by dragging inside a slice, or by dragging the handles.

Healing Brush Tool

  • It allows to fix image imperfections such as scratches, blemishes, etc by sampling the surrounding area or using a predefined pattern to blend the imperfections into the rest of the image.
  • The settings for healing brush tool are Brush size
    -     Blending mode 
    -     Source (Sampled from the image or pre-defined pattern)
    -     Aligned (remember the starting point between mouse clicks)

Blur, Sharpen, Smudge Tools

  • Blur Tool is used to soften the portions of the image causing it to  appear out of focus.
  • Sharpen Tool sharpens the edges in portions of an image. 
  • The Smudge Tool is like the Blur Tool, but it will slide the colors of an image around and blend them together.

Patch and Red Eye Tool

  • The patch tool retouches image using sampled pixels or pattern.
  • It is very similar to the Healing Brush tool except that it can define a selection where you want the effects applied to.
  • The red eye tool is used to remove the red eye images  with no changes in default settings with almost just two clicks.

Alpha Channels

  • Used to create selections and save them for later use.
  • The channel palette contains channels displayed similar to layers in layer palette
  • Create New Channel at the bottom of palette creates a new alpha channel.
  • Using painting or drawing tools create grayscale image representing the selection (white being selected, black being deselected)
  • To turn the alpha channel masterpiece into a selection, simply hold down Ctrl and click the channel's thumbnail. On returning to normal image view selection is still visible.

History Palette (Time Traveler)

  • Lists most recent steps made, and allows to undo the actions by rolling the image back to a previous state.
  • Ctrl-Z lets to undo & redo previous steps.
  • To save a "snapshot" of the document in order to revert back to it later, click on the small triangle on the top-right of the History palette and choose New Snapshot. It allows to save a snapshot of the whole document, the current layer, or merged layers.

Free Transform

  • It allows to apply transformations such as move, rotate, scale, skew, distort, perspective and wrap in one continuous operation.
  • To start free transform command go to Edit>Free Transform or press ctrl-T.
  • To apply the free transform double click or press enter and press esc to cancel.

Starting Free Transform

  • On starting Free Transform the selected area is surrounded by bounding box 
  •     with several handle at corner (A) and center of each side (B) and also reference point (C).

Use of Free Transform Handles

  • Corner Handle (A) : Used to Scale (both horizontal and vertical), Distort or perspective.
  • Side Handle (B) : Used to Scale horizontal, Scale vertical, Flip and Skew.
  • Reference point (C): Called Transformation Origin, as by default the origin is located in the center of the layer or selection. It can be moved to define the center point of transformation.
  • Rotate Area (D) : On moving the cursor anywhere outside bounding box (yellow area), it will turn to an arc symbol. It indicates beginning of rotation on dragging the mouse.

Move and Rotate

  • Move: Move the transforming object by move cursor in side bounding box.
  • Rotate: Rotate by dragging and position the pointer outside of the bounding border (D). It becomes a curved, two-sided arrow, and then drag

Flip and Scale

  • Flip: Image can be Flipped by dragging one handle (both Corner handle(A) and side handle (B)) past its opposite handle.
  • To perform uniform flip, right click in the working area and select Flip Horizontal or Flip Vertical.
  • Scale: Use both corner handle (A) and side handle (B) to perform scaling by moving the mouse to the handle until the cursor turns to double headed arrow and perform scaling horizontally or vertically.

Distort and Skew

  • Distort: Corner handle (A) is used to perform distortion by hold-down Ctrl key while dragging the handle.
  • Skew: Side handle (B) is used to perform skew by holding Ctrl key while dragging the handle.
  • To move selected handle and its opposite relative to reference point press Ctrl+Alt and drag.

Filters Basics

  • Filters are used to clean up or retouch images, apply special art effects, or create unique transformations using distortions and lighting effects.
  • They are applied to the active, visible layer or a selection.
  • Fade command (Edit > Fade) changes the opacity and blending mode of any filter, painting tool, erasing tool, or color adjustment

Types of Filters

  • Artistic Filters: achieve painterly and artistic effects
  • Blur Filters: soften selection (retouching).
  • Brush Strokes Filters: painterly or fine-arts look using brush and ink stroke effects.
  • Distort Filters: geometrically distort an image, creating 3D or other reshaping effects
  • Noise Filters: add or remove noise, or pixels with randomly distributed color levels
  • Pixelate Filters: sharply define a selection by clumping pixels of similar color values in cells
  • Render Filters: create 3D shapes, cloud patterns, refraction patterns, and simulated light reflections in an image
  • Sharpen Filters: focus blurred images by increasing the contrast of adjacent pixels
  • Sketch Filters: add texture to images, often for a 3D effect
  • Stylize Filters: produce painted or impressionistic effect on a selection by displacing pixels and by finding and heightening contrast in an image
  • Texture Filters:  simulate the appearance of depth or substance, or to add an organic look
  • Video Filters: contains the De-Interlace and NTSC Colors filters
  • Digimarc Filters: embed a digital watermark into an image to store copyright info
  • Vanishing Filters: preserves correct perspective in edits of images that contain perspective planes
  • Other Filters: create your own filters and use.
